# PART 1: Y COMBINATOR (YC)

## 1. Selection Criteria & Categories

### What Gets Selected
- **AI/ML dominates**: 88.5% of companies across W25-W26 batches are AI-related (up from ~40% in 2024)
- **Top categories funded (W25-W26 analysis of 793 companies)**:
  - AI Agents/Automation: 203 companies (highest)
  - AI Infrastructure/DevTools: 179
  - Generative AI/Content: 74
  - AI for Finance: 67
  - AI for Healthcare: 62
  - Robotics + AI: 46
- **B2B dominates**: 66% of companies are B2B; only 7% are pure consumer
- **Developer tools**: ~30% of S25 companies build developer tools/infrastructure
- **Crypto/Web3**: Relatively quiet in recent batches -- only a handful per batch (stablecoins, cross-border payments like BlindPay, Infinite, Karsa in W25)
- **Hardware**: Present but small; mostly robotics + AI category
- **Healthcare & Biotech**: ~7% of companies

### Revenue/MRR Requirements
- No hard revenue requirement -- YC accepts pre-revenue companies
- However, demonstrating traction significantly helps: MRR, active users, retention rates, engagement metrics
- Technical capabilities matter significantly for infrastructure/dev tool companies
- Consumer startups must show growth momentum or viral potential

### Team Size Preferences
- **2 co-founders is the sweet spot**: 67.8% of accepted companies (538/793)
- Solo founders: 14.4% (114 companies)
- 3 co-founders: 14.7% (117)
- 4+ co-founders: 2.5% (20)

### What Makes a Project Get Selected
- **Founding team quality** (most important factor)
- Clear problem-solution fit with evidence of user demand
- Market opportunity size
- Competitive differentiation
- Product traction or prototype viability
- Top founder backgrounds: Ex-FAANG (16.6%), Ivy League (10.1%), Ex-YC founder (10.1%), Stanford (7.5%), PhD (4.1%)

## 2. Patterns & Insights from Recent Batches

### W25 (Winter 2025)
- AI percentage: ~83.8%
- Focus areas: fintech, biotech, edtech, deep tech
- Several crypto startups focused on stablecoins and cross-border payments

### S25 (Summer 2025)
- **141 startups (88%) classified as AI-native** -- highest concentration in YC history
- Peak AI percentage: 92.9%
- ~169 startups total
- 30% build developer tools/infrastructure
- Other clusters: B2B productivity, healthcare/biotech, industrials/robotics, fintech

### W26 (Winter 2026)
- Demo Day: March 24, 2026 (yesterday as of this research)
- AI percentage settled around 87-90%

### Key Trends
- AI percentage grew from 83.8% (W25) to 92.9% (S25) to ~87-90% (W26)
- Pure fintech, crypto, Web3 are "relatively quiet" -- major cooling from 2022 peaks
- SF Bay Area dominance: 57% of companies are based there
- B2B heavily outweighs consumer (66% vs 7%)

### Solo Founders
- ~14.4% of YC companies are solo-founded
- Solo founders face higher barriers: must show stronger traction or technical capability
- Non-technical solo founders have acceptance rates below 0.6%
- YC accepts solo founders but the data shows a declining trend

### International Founders
- SF: 57%, Unknown/Remote: 27%, New York: 7%, Europe & International: ~6%
- International founders are welcome but SF-based companies dominate

## 3. Application Process

### Step-by-Step
1. **Submit online application** at apply.ycombinator.com -- includes company description, founder backgrounds, product demo/video, traction metrics
2. **Review period** -- YC reviews all submissions
3. **Interview invitation** -- promising candidates invited to 10-minute video interviews with YC partners
4. **Interview** -- conducted primarily via video conference; decisions typically made the same day
5. **Acceptance & funding** -- $500K SAFE invested immediately upon acceptance

- US incorporation typically required (Delaware C-Corp is standard)

## 6. Funding Details

### The Standard Deal (current as of 2026)
- **Total investment: $500,000**
- Split into two SAFE agreements:
  1. **$125,000 on a post-money SAFE** for **7% equity**
  2. **$375,000 on an uncapped MFN SAFE** (takes on terms of lowest cap SAFE issued before next equity round)
- Investment is NOT contingent on hitting milestones
- Funded immediately upon acceptance

### Additional Benefits
- Dedicated YC General Partner mentor
- Weekly meetings during batch
- Access to YC's investor network
- Ongoing support beyond 3-month batch
- Access to YC alumni community (5,000+ companies)
- Demo Day exposure to top-tier investors

### Follow-on Funding
- YC Continuity Fund invests in later-stage rounds
- Median Series A for YC companies is significantly higher than non-YC companies
- YC network effect is considered the primary value (introductions to investors, customers, talent)

# PART 2: a16z SPEEDRUN (Andreessen Horowitz)

## 1. Selection Criteria & Categories

### What Gets Selected
- **AI-dominant**: 69% of SR004 cohort was AI-native
- **Focus areas** (has expanded from gaming-only origin):
  - AI-first creative tools or infrastructure
  - AI agents for augmenting human labor
  - AI consumer apps (connect, create, play)
  - Next-gen AI marketing tools
  - Real-time collaboration/play/expression platforms
  - Games, virtual worlds, immersive storytelling
  - 3D, XR, entertainment
  - Developer infrastructure
  - Marketplaces
  - Social interaction platforms
- **Now a "horizontal program"**: founders of ANY type of startup can apply (no longer gaming-only)
- SR005 specifically focused on "AI and creative landscape -- tools, 3D, gaming, XR, entertainment"

### What Makes a Project Get Selected
The three big signals:
1. **Traction** -- the single strongest signal
2. **Team** -- world-class experience or a "cracked" combination of skills
3. **Relevance of experience** -- deep domain expertise matters more than a FAANG logo

### Additional Criteria
- Clarity of vision and execution capability
- Market opportunity
- Founder backgrounds prioritized over ideas at early stage
- "Proven capacity to go from zero to one without waiting for permission"
- Rapid execution capability
- Unique competitive insights

### Team Preferences
- Technical co-founder or equivalent expertise required
- Solo founders accepted but teams with complementary skills preferred
- Must be able to build and iterate on product internally

# PART 4: KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR APPLICANTS

## If You're an AI/Agents Startup
Both programs are excellent. YC has a higher concentration of AI companies (88%) and a larger alumni network. a16z Speedrun offers more money ($1M vs $500K) and $5M+ in credits.

## If You're a Solo Founder
YC is more proven for solo founders (~14% of each batch), though it's harder. a16z accepts solo founders but clearly prefers teams.

## If You're an Indian Founder
Both are viable. YC has a strong track record with 157 Indian companies (Razorpay, Zepto, Groww). a16z Speedrun explicitly lists India and provides visa support. Both require in-person SF attendance.

## If You're Non-AI (Hardware, Crypto, Web2 SaaS)
YC is the better bet -- it still accepts 11.5% non-AI companies across diverse categories. a16z Speedrun is more heavily weighted toward AI and creative/entertainment categories.

## Equity Consideration
- YC takes 7% for $125K (plus $375K on uncapped MFN SAFE) = effectively 7%+ depending on future terms
- a16z takes 10% for $500K upfront + $500K follow-on = more money but more dilution
- At early stage, the brand and network value of either program likely outweighs the equity cost

## Timing (as of March 2026)
- **YC S26**: Apply by May 4, 2026
- **a16z SR007**: Early apps open now, formal window April 2026
- Both are actionable RIGHT NOW
