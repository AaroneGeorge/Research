# 1. SEEDCAMP (London, Europe)

**Website:** https://seedcamp.com
**Apply:** https://seedcamp.com (click "Pitch Us")
**Type:** Pre-seed/Seed VC fund with accelerator support (NOT a traditional cohort-based accelerator)

## 1.1 Selection Criteria & Focus Areas

- **Sector agnostic** and "software-first investors" -- no particular vertical preferences
- Strong recent focus on **AI/ML** (18% of all venture investment; 70+ AI/ML commitments to date)
- Top portfolio sectors: Enterprise Applications (289 investments), FinTech (104 investments)
- Notable AI investments: Tattvam AI (semiconductor chip design automation), Peec AI (answer engine optimization), Synthesia ($4B valuation), EnzAI (AI governance), AskUI (AI automation)
- Also invests in: fintech (Revolut, Wise), gaming (Sorare), healthcare, marketplaces, SaaS
- **No revenue/MRR requirements** -- backs founders from pre-product/pre-launch through post-revenue
- They look for: dissatisfaction with the status quo, relentless default to action, deep customer understanding, prior 0-to-1 building experience

## 1.2 Patterns & Insights from Recent Cohorts

- Strong pattern of backing companies automating time-consuming, high-complexity activities in natural language (procurement RFPs, compliance, legal docs, code generation)
- 16 new investments in the last 12 months (as of Dec 2025)
- Portfolio includes 500+ companies, with unicorns: Revolut ($75B), Synthesia ($4B), UiPath, Wise, Sorare, Pleo, wefox, viz.ai
- Successful founders demonstrate: strong execution, deep industry knowledge, global ambition
- Access to Expert Collective (operators from Revolut, Wise, Stripe, Uber alumni)

## 1.3 Application Process

1. **Submit via website** -- fill in the form at seedcamp.com ("Pitch Us") or get referred by someone in their network
2. **Initial call** with one or more team members (evaluating technical skills, cultural alignment)
3. **Follow-up due diligence** -- second conversation or questions via WhatsApp/email
4. **45-minute Partner Pitch** with the full team (if strong fit)
5. **Decision within 2 weeks** of first meeting
6. They run **6 investment sprints per year** leading up to Investment Forums where companies pitch to Seedcamp + their LPs

**Application Timeline:** Rolling -- no fixed deadlines. They accept pitches year-round.
**Preparation Tip:** Start preparing materials 4-6 weeks before submission. Pitch deck should be max 20 slides covering: team's unique qualities, problem being solved, solution's market potential.

## 1.4 Acceptance Rate

- **~1% acceptance rate** -- approximately 37 out of 6,000 applications per year
- One of Europe's most selective seed funds
- They lead ~25 deals/year at pre-seed, syndicate 10-12 deals/year at seed

## 1.5 International & Indian Founders

- **Geographic focus: Europe and Israel**, or companies with a "distinctively European angle"
- No specific programs for Indian founders, BUT they recently led funding in **Tattvam AI** (founded by Bragadeesh Suresh Babu -- appears to be of Indian origin)
- Solo founders: Historically challenging (only 18% of seed-backed companies have solo founders), but landscape is evolving. Seedcamp doesn't explicitly prohibit it
- No explicit team size requirements mentioned

## 1.6 Funding Details

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **First check** | $350K - $1M |
| **Pre-seed ownership target** | 6-7% equity |
| **Pre-seed ticket size** | GBP 150-200K |
| **Seed ownership target** | 5%+ equity |
| **Seed ticket size** | GBP 200-500K |
| **Investment structure** | Convertible or equity (Seedsummit standard terms) |
| **Follow-on** | Yes, actively follows on through subsequent rounds to IPO |
| **Board seats** | Declines board seats; takes observer role through Series A |
| **Latest fund** | Fund VI -- $180M |

## 1.7 Key Resources

- Apply: https://seedcamp.com (Pitch Us link)
- How We Invest: https://seedcamp.com/how-we-invest/
- FAQs: https://seedcamp.com/faqs/
- Portfolio: https://seedcamp.com/our-companies/
- AI/ML investment thesis: https://seedcamp.com/views/ai-ml-and-seedcamps-commitments-to-date/

# 2. STARTUPWISEGUYS (Estonia/Europe-focused)

**Website:** https://startupwiseguys.com
**Apply:** https://startupwiseguys.com/all-programs/
**Type:** B2B-focused accelerator with multiple vertical programs across Europe

## 2.1 Selection Criteria & Focus Areas

- **B2B startups only** (B2B2C considered case-by-case; NO B2C)
- Verticals: **SaaS, Fintech, Cybersecurity & Data Privacy, Sustainability & Energy Tech, PropTech, Web3, Next-gen Connectivity, AI & Automation, Human-Machine Interfaces**
- **MVP required** for flagship accelerator
- **At least 2 co-founders** for flagship accelerator
- **Initial traction/MRR/revenues** needed for flagship programs
- For Founders Club (pre-accelerator): no idea or team required, 7+ years work experience preferred

## 2.2 Patterns & Insights

- 460+ portfolio companies, 880+ founders in network
- **70% survival rate** -- notably high
- 20+ exits from portfolio
- Founders from 60+ countries
- 12 investments made in 2025
- Strong European ecosystem focus with programs running from Estonia, Spain (Bilbao), and other European hubs
- Hybrid model: 80% online, 20% in-person

## 2.3 Application Process

### Flagship Accelerator (SaaS/Vertical programs):
1. **Submit application** on their website (reviewed first-come, first-served)
2. **Initial eligibility screening** (up to 2 weeks)
3. **Pitch day** presentation to partners and team
4. **Due diligence calls**: Tech call, Managing Director call, Partner/Expert call, Deal call
5. **Selection Bootcamp** participation
6. **Top 10-12 teams selected** per cohort
7. **Legal due diligence and compliance review**
8. **Agreement signing and fund transfer** (typically two equal tranches)
9. **14-week (5-month) acceleration program begins**

### Application Deadlines (2026):
| Program | Deadline |
|---------|----------|
| **Fintech** | February 1, 2026 |
| **Founders Club (Cohort 19)** | April 19, 2026 |
| **SaaS** | Check website for current dates |

## 2.4 Acceptance Rate

- Approximately **20-30 applicants reach final pitch stage** per program
- **10-12 teams selected** per cohort
- Exact application volume not publicly disclosed, but selectivity is moderate compared to top-tier programs

## 2.5 International & Indian Founders

- **No geographic restrictions** on applications
- Program activities on **EET (Eastern European Time)** -- important for Indian founders (IST is EET + 3.5 hours, so manageable)
- International community from 60+ countries
- **Companies must be incorporated in EU, USA, UK, or other fund-approved jurisdictions** -- SWG helps with establishing legal structures
- At least one founder required at onsite modules (Bilbao/Tallinn)
- No India-specific programs found, but Indian founders are eligible
- **Solo founders NOT eligible** for flagship accelerator (minimum 2 co-founders required)
- Solo founders CAN apply to **Founders Club** (pre-accelerator) to find co-founders

## 2.6 Funding Details

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Investment amount** | Up to EUR 100,000 |
| **Equity** | No fixed percentage -- negotiated case-by-case based on stage, market potential, revenue |
| **Investment instruments** | Equity agreements, Convertible Notes (18-24 months), or SAFE Notes |
| **Share type** | Preferred shares with standard VC rights |
| **Program service fee** | EUR 35,000 (invoiced AFTER investment transfer) |
| **Follow-on funding** | Up to EUR 250,000 available |
| **Bilbao bonus** | EUR 200,000 possible if registering legal entity in Bilbao |
| **Government grants** | Potential non-refundable grants up to EUR 125,000 in first year (Bilbao location) |

### Founders Club (Pre-Accelerator) Costs:
- Individuals: EUR 1,000 + VAT
- Teams: EUR 1,500 + VAT
- CTOs/Developers: EUR 500 + VAT
- If selected for investment after: EUR 35,000 (for EUR 100K) or EUR 20,000 (for EUR 50K)

## 2.7 Program Structure (Flagship)

**Duration: 5 months, 4 modules**

| Module | Weeks | Focus |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Module 1 | 1-4 | KPIs, Company Building, Market Engineering (IN-PERSON) |
| Module 2 | 5-8 | Communication, Sales, Product Sprint |
| Module 3 | 9-12 | Sales, Marketing, Growth, Mentor Sessions |
| Module 4 | 13-14 | Fundraising, Investor Readiness, Demo Day (IN-PERSON) |

- 350+ mentors in network
- ~100 mentors across workshops and sprints
- Weekly 1:1 sessions with assigned lead coaches
- Access to 200+ investors at Demo Day
- Post-program: monthly reporting for 2 years, quarterly thereafter

# 3. BOOST VC (San Mateo, California)

**Website:** https://www.boost.vc
**Apply:** https://www.boost.vc (intake form) or email info@boost.vc
**Type:** Pre-seed VC fund + accelerator for "Sci-Fi" deep tech

## 3.1 Selection Criteria & Focus Areas

- **Thesis: "Accelerating the Sci-Fi Future"** -- technologies that look like science fiction
- **Sectors:** Aerospace/Space, Robotics, Nuclear/Energy, Climate, Biotech/Longevity, AI/ML, Crypto/Blockchain, VR/AR, Defense, Maritime, Materials Science
- **Generalist within deep tech** -- both hardware AND software
- Evaluates three key areas:
  1. **Team**: "Show us your energy for what you are doing"
  2. **Market Timing**: Evidence of social, technical, or regulatory pressure driving change
  3. **Vision Scale**: They fund "bets no other investor is willing to make"
- Founder profile: "Great people doing important things" -- unique world perspective, confidence, energy, authenticity, resourcefulness ("cockroaches" who adapt and survive)
- Adam Draper believes "great companies often sound dumb at the beginning"
- Values heads-down execution over hype; "quiet compounding"

## 3.2 Patterns & Insights from Recent Cohorts

- 20 investments made in 2025
- 70+ startups invested in annually
- **6 unicorns in portfolio**: Radiant Nuclear, K2 Space, 21.co, Coinbase, and others
- Portfolio: 364 tech companies, 226 Enterprise (B2B), 222 Software, 208+ Consumer (B2C)
- Recent investments heavily skew toward: nuclear energy, space tech, robotics, manufacturing
- Radiant Nuclear & Matta are latest investments
- Notable portfolio: Coinbase, Deepgram, Radiant Nuclear, K2 Space, Starfish Space
- **90% of deals come through referrals** -- warm intros are critical
- Fund IV closed at $87.6M (September 2025)
- Total AUM: $400M+

## 3.3 Application Process

1. **Submit intake form** on boost.vc website OR get a referral from a trusted source
2. **Initial call** -- decision on next steps within 1 week
3. **1-2 follow-up meetings** with team members (1-2 weeks)
4. **Final decision and operations onboarding**
5. Total process: approximately 2-4 weeks

**Rolling applications** -- no fixed batch deadlines for main program
**Bio Residency** has specific deadlines (e.g., July 31 deadline for recent batch)

## 3.4 Acceptance Rate

- Not publicly disclosed
- Invests in ~70+ startups per year (roughly 1 per week)
- Given high volume of applications and brand recognition, likely in the **3-5% range** (estimated)
- Warm referrals significantly increase odds

## 3.5 International & Indian Founders

- **Invests globally** -- explicitly states they invest worldwide
- No India-specific programs found
- **Relocation consideration**: Free housing at Draper University in San Mateo area is a benefit, suggesting preference for founders who can be on-site
- Bio Residency requires willingness to relocate to San Mateo
- International founders face logistical challenges (visas, relocation) but are welcome
- Solo founders: Not explicitly prohibited; they evaluate on team quality
- Semi-annual Founder Summits for portfolio community

## 3.6 Funding Details

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Standard pre-seed check** | $500K |
| **Flexible range** | $100K - $1M |
| **Fellowship (early-stage)** | $50K |
| **Bio Residency** | $200-250K for 8-9% equity |
| **Investment structure** | SAFE (Standard) |
| **Target valuations** | $3M - $7M (sub-$1M rounds) |
| **Follow-on** | Portfolio companies have raised $5B+ in subsequent rounds |
| **Free housing** | Available at Draper University (San Mateo) |
| **Portfolio perks** | 50+ service provider discounts |
| **VC introductions** | 1,000+ annually |
| **Portfolio network** | 400+ companies |

### Program Tracks:
- **Pre-Seed ($500K):** For companies ready for first institutional round
- **Fellowship ($50K):** For founders just getting started (apply online)
- **Bio Residency:** 2-week immersive + $200-250K investment, San Mateo

# 4. PIONEER (Fully Remote / Online)

**Website:** https://pioneer.app
**Type:** Fully remote accelerator for "ambitious outsiders"
**IMPORTANT: Pioneer STOPPED making new investments in 2024**

## 4.1 Current Status (Critical Update)

- **Pioneer stopped making new investments in March 2024** (last investment: Tracecat, March 27, 2024)
- The original "Tournament" format ended in April 2023
- The program may still accept applications for community/accelerator benefits, but **no new funding is being deployed**
- Founded in 2018 by **Daniel Gross** (ex-YC partner) and **Rishi Narang**, backed by Stripe and Marc Andreessen

## 4.2 Historical Selection Criteria (for reference)

- **No sector restrictions** -- accepted all types of projects
- Designed for founders in the "earliest days" of building
- Key criteria: ambition, weekly progress, project quality
- Original tournament system: weekly peer-review of progress updates with points-based voting
- Top 50 on Global Leaderboard reviewed by experts
- New Pioneers chosen every 1-2 weeks
- Valued "ambitious outsiders" -- founders from non-traditional backgrounds and locations

## 4.3 How Pioneer Worked (Historical)

### The Tournament (ended April 2023):
1. **Sign up** on pioneer.app
2. **Submit weekly progress updates** detailing project progress
3. **Peer review**: Community members compare pairs of participants and vote
4. **Leaderboard ranking**: Points-based system determines rankings
5. **Top 50 reviewed by experts**: Project description, past updates, and team evaluated
6. **Selection**: New Pioneers chosen every 1-2 weeks

### The Accelerator Program:
1. **3-month remote program** with regular meetings with Pioneer team
2. **External advisor sessions** and fellow founder meetings
3. **Group and 1:1 sessions** covering: customer development, product, growth, fundraising
4. **1-month in-person Summit** in San Francisco
5. **Optional investment** at the end

## 4.4 Acceptance Rate (Historical)

- Funded **150+ companies** over its lifetime
- **300+ founders** in **50+ countries**
- Companies worth **$1B+ in aggregate**
- Companies raised **$400M+** from Sequoia, a16z, General Catalyst, YC, Khosla
- Notable alumni: Railway ($120M+), Roboflow ($60M+), Slash ($60M+), E2B ($30M+), Iron Fish ($30M+)

## 4.5 International & Indian Founders

- **Designed specifically for global/international founders** -- this was Pioneer's core thesis
- "Making opportunity available for people all around the world"
- Founders from **50+ countries** in the network
- **Solo founders explicitly welcome** -- mentorship specifically valuable for solo entrepreneurs
- The fully remote model was built to eliminate geographic barriers
- Co-founder Rishi Narang is of Indian descent
- No visa or relocation requirements (fully remote until optional SF Summit)

## 4.6 Funding Details (Historical)

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Initial investment** | $20,000 at $2M valuation (optional) |
| **Level 2** | $100,000 (for advancing Pioneers) |
| **Level 3** | $1,000,000 (for top performers) |
| **Equity structure** | Based on valuation, not fixed percentage |
| **Additional perks** | Free company formation via Stripe Atlas |
| **Network** | 300+ founders, 50+ countries |

## 4.7 The Remote Model (Key Differentiator)

Pioneer was specifically built as a **fully remote, online-first** accelerator:
- No physical location requirement
- Weekly virtual check-ins
- Community-driven peer accountability
- Time-zone flexible
- Designed to find talent that traditional accelerators miss (outside Silicon Valley, outside traditional networks)
- Daniel Gross described it as more of a "startup generator" than a traditional accelerator
- Ideal for founders in developing countries, remote locations, or those who cannot relocate

# RECOMMENDATIONS FOR INDIAN FOUNDERS

## Best Options (Active):
1. **Boost VC** -- If building deep tech (AI, crypto, space, bio, robotics). Global investment, $500K checks. Need warm intro (90% referral-based). May need to relocate or visit San Mateo.

2. **Seedcamp** -- If building software/SaaS/AI with European market angle. Very prestigious, $350K-$1M. Must have European connection or angle. Sector agnostic.

3. **StartupWiseGuys** -- If building B2B SaaS/Fintech with 2+ co-founders and MVP. Lower funding (EUR 100K) but structured program. Must incorporate in EU/USA/UK. Next deadline: Founders Club April 19, 2026.

## For Solo Indian Founders:
- StartupWiseGuys is NOT an option (requires 2+ co-founders for flagship)
- Seedcamp and Boost VC are possible but harder for solo founders
- StartupWiseGuys Founders Club (pre-accelerator) accepts solos for EUR 1,000 to help find co-founders
- Pioneer was the best option for solo/remote international founders, but is currently paused

## Key Tips:
- For Boost VC: Get a warm referral; lead with technical breakthrough; show founder-market fit
- For Seedcamp: Have a clear European market strategy; emphasize 0-to-1 experience
- For StartupWiseGuys: Have MVP + traction + co-founder; B2B only
- For all: Strong pitch deck, clear problem-solution, demonstrable execution ability
