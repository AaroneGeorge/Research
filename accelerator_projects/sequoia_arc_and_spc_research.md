## PART 1: SEQUOIA ARC

### 1. Overview

Sequoia Arc is Sequoia Capital's bi-annual open call for **pre-seed and seed stage founders**. Launched in March 2022, Arc is designed to accelerate a startup's path to product-market fit (PMF) through an intensive workshop program. It is NOT a traditional accelerator -- it is a "Company Building Immersion" where becoming a Sequoia portfolio company is the core outcome.

- **Program Duration:** ~5 weeks (previously 7-8 weeks; shortened over time)
- **Format:** Hybrid -- 3 in-person weeks (kickoff in NYC/Bay Area, mid-program field trip, final presentations) with remote sessions in between
- **Cohort Size:** ~10-15 companies per cohort
- **Frequency:** Twice a year (Spring and Fall)
- **Eligibility:** Startups headquartered in the **Americas and Europe** only

---

### 2. Selection Criteria

#### What Gets Selected:

**Dominant sectors from analyzed cohorts (26 companies tracked):**
- **AI/ML** -- Largest category (~30%): Squint (AI/AR), Arcwise (AI copilot for Google Sheets), Mirage (AI video generation), Synativ (generative AI for computer vision), Telescope (AI sales prospecting), Twain (AI outreach)
- **Developer Tools/Infrastructure** (~15%): Zeet (cloud infrastructure), Baselime (serverless observability), Popsink (real-time ETL)
- **Fintech** (~15%): Breeze (card/crypto payments), Otto Finance (financial wellness), Xelda (business financial infrastructure)
- **Healthcare** (~8%): Roon (health navigation), Summer Health (pediatric telehealth)
- **Cybersecurity** (~8%): Akeero (cloud threat detection), Fides (governance/compliance)
- **Creator/Consumer** (~12%): Flagship (boutique commerce), Bento (portfolios), Hallway (video conferencing)
- **Insurance, Travel, Productivity** also represented

**Key Pattern:** AI-augmented B2B software and vertical SaaS dominate. Pure consumer plays are rare. Crypto/Web3 representation is minimal (only Breeze had a crypto component).

#### Revenue/MRR Requirements:
- **No strict requirements on traction or revenue.** Sequoia explicitly states they accept founders at the earliest stage with "just an idea" all the way to founders with a working product and early traction.
- The program is designed for the **-1 to 0** and **0 to 1** stages.

#### Team Size:
- No explicit minimum or maximum team size stated.
- Both solo founders and teams have been accepted, though co-founded teams appear more common in cohort listings.
- Sequoia values "Founder-Market Fit" -- deep domain expertise in the space you're building in.

#### What Makes You Get Selected:
1. **Outlier founder profile** -- Sequoia explicitly says "outliers wanted." They look for what makes you uniquely qualified.
2. **Founder-Market Fit** -- An industry edge that makes you more likely to achieve PMF in your category.
3. **Bold ambition** -- Category-creating vision, not incremental improvements.
4. **Customer understanding** -- Successful applicants had been "speaking to prospects since day one."
5. **Deep domain experience** -- One accepted founder noted "both founders had a decade of experience in the industry."
6. **Alignment with Sequoia's beliefs** -- Understanding what Sequoia values and demonstrating alignment.

---

### 3. Application Process

#### Step-by-Step:

1. **Apply online** at [sequoiacap.com/arc/apply](https://sequoiacap.com/arc/apply/) -- Submit application with company details, founder background, and vision
2. **Review period** -- Sequoia reviews all applications (typically 2-3 weeks after deadline)
3. **Interview invitation** -- Selected applicants receive a 30-minute interview invitation
4. **Interview** -- Described as "a taste of the YC interview: no bullshit, high flow" -- fast-paced, direct questioning
5. **Decision** -- Approximately 3 weeks after interview, accepted founders are notified
6. **Term sheet** -- Funding terms are negotiated on a company-specific basis
7. **Program begins** -- 5-week hybrid intensive starts

#### Alternative Entry Path:

#### Repeat Applications Welcome:
If rejected, Sequoia encourages you to apply again. Prior cohorts include companies that were rejected the first time. They ask that you explain what has changed (pivot, new traction, new insight).

### 4. Acceptance Rate

- **Estimated <2%** -- possibly lower
- The inaugural European cohort selected **17 companies from thousands of applicants**
- Cohort sizes are intentionally small (~10-15 companies)
- One founder account mentions ~300 founders attending a single meet-and-greet event, suggesting very high interest
- The program has become increasingly competitive as Sequoia's brand attracts top global talent

### 5. International / Indian Founders

**Geographic restriction:** Sequoia Arc is ONLY open to startups headquartered in the **Americas and Europe/UK**. India and Asia are NOT eligible.

**However:**
- There is no citizenship/nationality restriction -- the requirement is based on where the startup is incorporated/headquartered, not the founder's origin.
- An Indian-origin founder could apply if their company is headquartered in the US, UK, or Europe.
- Sequoia has separate regional entities:
  - **Sequoia Capital (US/Europe)** -- runs Arc
  - **Peak XV Partners** (formerly Sequoia India/SEA) -- runs Surge program for India/SEA founders
  - **HongShan** (formerly Sequoia China) -- separate entity

**Significant European presence in cohorts:** Companies from London, Berlin, Munich, Dublin, Cork (Ireland), and Paris have been accepted, showing strong European representation.

### 7. Program Structure (What You Get)

**Six core workshop sessions:**
1. **Founder & Culture Story** -- Define vision and founding narrative
2. **Customer Story** -- Articulate the problem and customer
3. **Product Story** -- Mission, metrics, and product direction
4. **Go-to-Market Story** -- Sales, positioning, distribution
5. **Business Story** -- Business model and strategic alignment
6. **PMF Framework** -- Weekly validation using Sequoia's proprietary PMF framework

**Additional benefits:**
- 1:1 time with Sequoia partners (Jess Lee, etc.)
- Guest lecturers from Sequoia portfolio (OpenAI, Stripe, Notion, Zipline founders)
- Access to **Ampersand** -- Sequoia's digital hub and network rolodex connecting all portfolio founders
- Builder's program connecting to industry experts
- Final presentation event (focused on storytelling/recruiting, NOT investor pitch)
- ~8 hours/week commitment, though engaged founders spend more

## PART 2: SOUTH PARK COMMONS (SPC) FOUNDER FELLOWSHIP

### 1. Overview

South Park Commons (SPC) is a community and investment fund founded by **Ruchi Sanghvi** (first female engineer at Facebook) and **Aditya Agarwal** (former Dropbox CTO). The Founder Fellowship is their flagship program designed to help founders navigate the **"-1 to 0" journey** -- the period before you have a clear company, when you're exploring ideas, building conviction, and forming teams.

- **Program Duration:** ~8-week bootcamp + open-ended residency (as long as you need)
- **Format:** In-person at SPC offices in **San Francisco, New York City, or Bangalore**
- **Cohort Size:** Purposefully small (~14 founders); 1 partner for every 2 companies
- **Frequency:** Twice a year (Spring and Fall)
- **Eligibility:** Open **globally** -- any country, any sector
- **Founded:** SPC community since 2014; Founder Fellowship launched more recently

**Key differentiator:** SPC explicitly says this is "NOT an accelerator." There are no demo days, no fixed end dates, and no pressure to rush. The focus is on finding the right problem, not speed-running to a pitch.

---

### 2. Selection Criteria

#### What Gets Selected:

**SPC is sector-agnostic.** They have funded companies across:
- Software, AI/ML, and AI research
- Hardware and robotics
- Biotech and space tech
- Enterprise security
- Fintech (Comun, Ascend, Orb)
- Developer tools (Render, Baseten, Replit)
- Consumer (Luma Labs, Gamma)
- Insurance, video/content creation

**No single niche dominates.** SPC is explicitly looking for "ambitious technologists" across ALL sectors.

#### What They Look For:

1. **Ambitious technologists** -- People with the ability to "build at the frontier"
2. **Idea generation capacity** -- You DON'T need an idea to apply, but you must demonstrate HOW you think about generating ideas (technical approaches, research interests, market insights)
3. **"Illegible" or "heretical" ideas** -- They seek ideas with high technical risk that others might dismiss
4. **Building/prototyping ability** -- Especially critical for solo founders. "Strong bias for applicants who have the skills to build and prototype"
5. **Venture-scale ambition** -- The program explicitly requires commitment to venture-backed business models; not for bootstrappers or lifestyle businesses
6. **Non-traditional founders actively encouraged** -- SPC does NOT require traditional Silicon Valley pedigree

#### Revenue/MRR Requirements:
- **None.** The program is designed for pre-revenue, pre-product, and even pre-idea founders.
- You need a "high-conviction north star" (market, idea, or product) -- not revenue.

#### Team Size:
- **Solo founders are welcome** but must demonstrate ability to build/prototype independently
- **Co-founders:** Both must apply separately, noting the relationship in each application
- SPC can help solo founders find co-founders through the community
- Previous fellows have found co-founders within the SPC community

---

### 3. Application Process

#### Step-by-Step:

1. **Apply online** at [southparkcommons.com/apply](https://www.southparkcommons.com/apply)
2. **Internal review** -- SPC reviews applications (typically 1-3 weeks, but up to 4 weeks for fellowship deadlines)
3. **Interview invitation** -- All applicants hear back about interview status by the notification date
4. **Interview** -- Described as an opportunity "to learn more about you" (less adversarial than YC-style)
5. **Decision** -- Offers extended
6. **Bootcamp kickoff** -- Starts with a private retreat in Northern California, then moves to SPC offices

#### Two Tracks at SPC:

1. **Member Residency** -- 6-month exploratory period. No idea needed. ~70% are aspiring founders, ~30% are researchers/academics/builders. Rolling admissions with monthly cohorts.
2. **Founder Fellowship** -- More structured. Requires "early team and a high-conviction north star." Twice yearly with specific deadlines. This is the funded track ($1M).

---

### 4. Acceptance Rate

- **Not publicly disclosed.** SPC does not publish acceptance rate statistics.
- Cohorts are "purposefully small" (estimated ~14 founders per cohort)
- The program has become increasingly competitive as SPC's reputation has grown
- Over the past decade, SPC has funded **250+ companies** from pre-idea to seed stage and beyond
- Rolling admissions for Member Residency means they "are more likely to make offers when there are more slots available" -- suggesting applying early in the cycle helps
- **Estimated range:** Given the program's growing profile and small cohort sizes, acceptance is likely in the **5-10% range**, though this is an estimate based on program characteristics, not official data

### 5. International / Indian Founders

**SPC is one of the BEST options for Indian founders among top-tier programs.**

#### Key advantages:
- **Bangalore office** -- SPC operates an office in Bangalore, India. Fellows can do the bootcamp from Bangalore.
- **Globally open** -- Applications are accepted from any country
- **Indian-origin leadership** -- Co-founders Ruchi Sanghvi and Aditya Agarwal are of Indian origin; team includes Arian Agrawal, Prateek Mehta, Ankit Chowdhary, and Gopal Raman
- **No visa sponsorship** -- SPC cannot directly sponsor visas, but they evaluate international applicants on a case-by-case basis and welcome discussion about visa circumstances
- **Diverse alumni** -- Examples of immigrant founders (Comun founders are from Mexico; international backgrounds well-represented)

#### Locations for bootcamp:
Fellows must work in-person from one of three offices:
1. **San Francisco** (primary hub)
2. **New York City**
3. **Bangalore, India**

This makes SPC uniquely accessible for India-based founders compared to most US-based programs that require SF/NYC presence.

### 7. Program Structure (What You Get)

**Phase 1: Bootcamp (~8 weeks)**
- Starts with private retreat in Northern California
- In-person at SPC office (SF, NYC, or Bangalore)
- Weekly partner mentorship (multiple sessions per week)
- Bi-weekly presentations creating "forcing function for forward progress"
- Customer discovery, product development, technical scoping
- Branding and launch support

- Ends with optional Demo Faire for investors

**Phase 2: Residency (flexible duration)**
- Full access to SPC offices
- Continued partner support
- Programming and events
- Fundraising support
- Lasts "as long as you need to reach conviction and get ready to raise your next round"

**Additional benefits:**
- **Partner ratio:** 1 partner for every 2 companies
- **Peer cohort:** ~14 founders working simultaneously
- **Network:** "Connected to literally everyone in technology"
- **Co-founder matching** through the SPC community
- **No fixed end date** -- no artificial pressure

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

### For Indian Founders:
- **SPC is the clear winner** -- Bangalore office, Indian-origin leadership, globally open applications
- **Sequoia Arc is NOT accessible** unless you incorporate in the US or Europe first
- For Sequoia's India programs, look at **Peak XV Partners Surge** (formerly Sequoia India)

### For AI/Agent Builders:
- Both programs are highly receptive to AI companies
- Sequoia Arc has ~30% AI companies in cohorts; SPC is sector-agnostic but has notable AI alumni (Luma, Imbue, Goodfire)
- SPC's $900K+ in AI credits (OpenAI, Anthropic) is particularly valuable for AI companies

### For Solo Founders:
- **SPC is more explicitly solo-founder friendly** -- they openly accept solo founders with building skills
- **Sequoia Arc** does not explicitly address solo founders but teams appear more common

### For Pre-idea Stage:
- **SPC is designed for this** -- no idea required, the program helps you find one
- **Sequoia Arc** prefers founders with at least a direction, though "just an idea" is acceptable

### Application Strategy:
- Both programs run Spring and Fall cycles; apply to BOTH if eligible
- Apply early in the cycle (especially SPC, which fills slots)
- If rejected, reapply -- both programs accept repeat applicants
- For Sequoia Arc: emphasize your unique "outlier" qualities and founder-market fit
- For SPC: emphasize your technical ability, ambition, and capacity for idea generation
