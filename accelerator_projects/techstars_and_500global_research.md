# PART 1: TECHSTARS

## 1. Selection Criteria

### What Categories/Niches Get Selected
Techstars is **industry-agnostic** at its core but runs **specialized vertical programs** alongside general ones. Based on current active programs and recent cohorts:

**Strong Focus Areas (2025-2026):**
- **AI/ML** -- Heavily represented. Techstars AI Health Baltimore is dedicated to AI in healthcare. Multiple AI startups across cohorts (Avido AI, Brixely, Bundled AI, Ember AI, FairAI).
- **SaaS/Enterprise Software** -- Consistently represented in NYC, Boulder, and SF cohorts
- **Fintech** -- ABN AMRO + Techstars Future of Finance program (Amsterdam)
- **Healthcare/Biotech** -- Northwestern Medicine & Techstars Healthcare (Chicago), Techstars Healthcare powered by Permanente Medicine (Washington D.C.)
- **Space/Deep Tech** -- Techstars Space (Los Angeles)
- **Web3/Crypto** -- Dedicated Techstars Web3 Accelerator (selected 6 startups from 650 applications in 2025 class; previous classes had 12 from ~750 applications)
- **Climate/Clean Tech** -- Techstars Alabama EnergyTech, Techstars WaterTech & Sustainability
- **Food Tech** -- Techstars Future of Food powered by Ecolab (Minneapolis)
- **Workforce/HR Tech** -- Techstars Workforce Development (Denver)
- **Media/Entertainment** -- USC and Techstars (Los Angeles) focuses on bioscience, engineering, AI, healthcare, deep tech, and media technology

**Hardware:** Not a primary focus but not excluded. Most cohorts lean software/SaaS.

### Revenue/MRR Requirements
- **No strict revenue requirements.** Companies with zero revenue have been accepted alongside those making millions.
- Traction metrics ARE evaluated -- they ask for 6 months of revenue/customer/user growth KPIs with month-over-month growth
- Pre-revenue companies are accepted if they can demonstrate strong problem validation and team quality

### Team Size Preferences
- **Strongly prefers teams of 2+ co-founders**
- Solo founders CAN be accepted but it is rare -- "exceptional solo founders with strong technical or domain expertise have been accepted"
- Solo founders must demonstrate ability to attract talent, advisors, and support
- They ask whether each founder is full-time or part-time

### What Makes a Project Get Selected
**#1 criterion is TEAM QUALITY.** Beyond that:
1. Why are YOU the right people to solve this problem?
2. Market size and timing -- why now?
3. Traction/momentum (even if pre-revenue)
4. Competitive differentiation
5. Coachability and openness to feedback
6. Full-time commitment from at least one founder
7. Customer acquisition strategy clarity
8. Ambition for global impact

## 3. Application Process

### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Create Profile (3-5 mins)**
- Name, title, email, location, LinkedIn, phone
- Full-time commitment status

**Step 2: Company Application (20-30 mins)**
- Startup name, URL, year founded
- Problem description and solution
- Incorporation details
- Industry selection
- **3 required videos**: product demo, pitch deck walkthrough, team introduction
- Team strengths and composition
- Current traction metrics (6 months of data)
- Revenue and funding status
- Revenue model
- Target audience
- Competitor landscape and differentiation
- Market timing rationale
- Customer acquisition approach
- Major upcoming milestones

**Step 3: Program Selection (3-5 mins)**
- Select up to 3 preferred accelerator programs
- Or leave unselected and let Techstars recommend matches

**Step 4: Screening & Review**
- Applications reviewed by program Managing Directors
- Timeline: 2-3 months from deadline to final decision

**Step 5: Video Interview**
- Questions include: team composition, conflicts resolved, business roadmap
- "What will you accomplish in 3 months? 1 year?"
- "What are the lifetime capital requirements of your company?"

**Step 6: Final Selection**
- In-person or virtual meetings for finalists
- Final decisions communicated

## 6. Funding Details

### Current Investment Terms (effective Fall 2025)

| Component | Amount | Details |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Uncapped MFN SAFE | $200,000 | Post-money Safe, no valuation cap |

- **40% conversion rate to Series A** (4 in 10 companies raise significant round post-graduation)
- Companies raise $1-2M on average post Demo Day
- Techstars operates venture funds that make follow-on investments in alumni companies
- 3,500+ startups backed, 300+ exits

### Cost to Founders
- **No fee to join** a Techstars accelerator program

# PART 2: 500 GLOBAL (formerly 500 Startups)

## 1. Selection Criteria

### What Categories/Niches Get Selected
500 Global is **sector-agnostic** but with clear preferences visible in recent cohorts:

**Accepted Verticals:**
- **AI/ML** -- Increasingly dominant in recent batches
- **Enterprise SaaS** -- Core focus area
- **Fintech** -- Strong historical focus
- **Marketplaces** -- Consistently represented
- **Consumer Tech** -- Accepted but less favored than B2B
- **Media/Creator Economy** -- New Creators Ventures Accelerator launched (inaugural cohort attracted 1,131 applications from 70+ countries)
- **Healthcare** -- Growing focus
- **Climate/Sustainability** -- Sustainable Innovation Program exists
- **Crypto/Web3** -- Accepted but not a primary focus
- **Hardware** -- Less common; software-first bias

**Regional Programs:**
- Sanabil Accelerator (MENA region)
- Southeast Asia program (300+ portfolio companies across 9 countries)
- Various regional programs globally

### Revenue/MRR Requirements
- **No strict revenue requirement** -- pre-seed and seed stage accepted
- Product must be in development or market testing (beyond idea stage)
- If pre-traction: must emphasize problem urgency and team domain expertise
- Real user metrics, pilot customers, or strong market validation are highlighted in evaluation
- They ask for 6 months of revenue data (actual USD) if available
- Monthly burn rate and cash balance are requested

### Team Size Preferences
- **At least one full-time founder** is required
- Teams preferred but not strictly required
- Application asks for full-time and part-time team member counts
- Must be prepared to relocate to Palo Alto/Silicon Valley for 4 months

### What Makes a Project Get Selected
1. **Traction or strong early indicators** of market demand
2. **Team complementarity** and domain expertise
3. **Execution speed** and momentum -- rapid prototyping and iteration
4. **Global scaling potential** -- must demonstrate how product can scale/adapt to multiple regions
5. **Market size** and timing
6. **Diversity and market innovation** prioritized
7. Vision for international expansion
8. Competitive differentiation

## 3. Application Process

### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Online Application (52 fields)**
Submit through https://flagship.aplica.500.co/

Required materials:
- Pitch deck (shared link)
- Cap table (latest version)
- Product demo video (optional but recommended)
- LinkedIn profiles for all co-founders
- 6-month revenue history (actual USD)
- 6-month customer/user count history

- Business model description (150 words max)
- Go-to-market channels
- Problem statement
- Competitive landscape
- Target customer regions (max 3)
- Funding history

**Step 2: Screening**
- Applications reviewed on rolling basis

**Step 3: Interview**
- Shortlisted founders have calls or in-person interviews
- Evaluates product viability, market fit, and team dynamics
- Multiple rounds possible

**Step 4: Selection & Offer**
- Accepted companies receive investment terms
- Must agree to relocate to Silicon Valley

- **Rolling admissions -- 365 days/year**
- No fixed application deadlines for Flagship
- On-time deadline for current cycle: **May 4, 2026 at 8:00 PM PT** (decisions by June 5)
- Late submissions continue to receive consideration

### Upcoming Batch
- **Batch 37**: Starts Q2 2026
- **Next Flagship cohort after that**: October 2026 - February 2027

### Tips for Acceptance
1. Demonstrate traction with real metrics, not vanity numbers
2. Emphasize global ambitions
3. Highlight team strength with complementary skills
4. Showcase execution speed
5. If rejected, reapply after demonstrable progress -- re-applications are welcomed

# KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR AN INDIAN/INTERNATIONAL FOUNDER

1. **Both accept international founders** -- 500 Global explicitly states 30-40% of cohorts are international. Techstars runs programs in 20+ cities across multiple countries.

2. **No strict revenue requirement** at either -- but traction/metrics significantly help. Show whatever you have: users, pilots, LOIs, revenue.

3. **Team > Solo** -- Both strongly prefer teams of 2+. Solo founders can get in but face a higher bar.

4. **AI/SaaS is hot** -- Both accelerators are heavily selecting AI-enabled startups across verticals. Pure AI infra and AI applied to verticals both work.

5. **Techstars offers more money net** -- $220K with no fee vs $112.5K net at 500 Global. But 500 Global's $500K follow-on rights are valuable.

6. **Apply to both** -- With ~1% acceptance rates, cast a wide net. 500 Global allows re-application with improved metrics.

7. **Relocation required** -- Both require physical presence. Plan for US visa if applying to US-based programs. Techstars Anywhere is the only remote option.

8. **Upcoming deadlines:**
   - 500 Global: May 4, 2026 (on-time for current cycle)
   - Techstars Fall 2026: June 10, 2026

9. **For Indian founders specifically:**
   - 500 Global has an India Batch 10 program (April-June 2026)
   - Techstars APAC programs offer lower investment ($100K vs $220K)
   - Both have track record of accepting Indian founders

10. **Crypto/Web3** -- Techstars has a dedicated Web3 accelerator. 500 Global accepts crypto but no dedicated program.
